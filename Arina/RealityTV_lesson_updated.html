Speaking · use the adjectives話そう

answer in 2-3 full sentences
4
Choose any prompt below and answer it out loud using the adjective in bold.
Try to give an example from a real show. The shows at the bottom are conversation starters.
brilliant
What's the most brilliant K-drama you've ever seen? What made it so good — the writing, the acting, the visuals?
dull
Have you ever started a show that everyone said was amazing — but you found it really dull? Which one and why?
moving
Tell me about a scene that genuinely moved you. Maybe you cried, maybe you just sat there for ten minutes after the episode ended.
funny
What's the funniest variety show or anime moment you've ever seen? Try to retell it so it makes sense.
scary
Do you actually enjoy scary shows? Why or why not? Name one you couldn't finish (or one you couldn't stop watching).
original
Which Asian show feels really original to you — something you can't compare to anything else? What makes it unique?
inspiring
Has any show ever inspired you to try something new — drawing, dance, language, food? What did it make you want to do?
violent
Some hit shows are pretty violent (Squid Game, All of Us Are Dead). Does that bother you, or is it part of the story for you?
annoying
Which TV character do you find most annoying — and yet you keep watching? What is it about them?
informative
Have you ever watched a documentary or vlog that was genuinely informative — about K-pop, Japan, food, history? Tell me about it.

Read · Reality TV Blog読もう

click any word to see a Russian meaning
REALITY TV Blog
News Sport Television Weather More ▾
1. "Reality TV? Not for me — I'd rather watch something more serious." That's what plenty of people say. But a recent survey by OnePoll discovered that one in four people in the UK regularly watches a reality show. Maybe that isn't really surprising when you think about how many shows there are to choose from — hundreds of them. Many popular examples involve people singing, dancing or cooking. Lately, though, much stranger formats have appeared: a competition where men try to grow the longest beard (Whisker Wars), celebrities completing wild challenges in the Australian jungle (I'm a Celebrity… Get Me Out of Here!), or a group of 40 children who lived together for 40 days with no adults at all (Kid Nation).
2. But how real are the people in reality TV shows? It depends. Some shows actually use professional actors. Others use ordinary members of the public, because they cost much less than actors. But even when the people are real, the situations are usually carefully written and planned in advance. And even when real people are talking about real situations, it's easy to edit their words and make them say something different. In spite of all of this, a survey discovered that 42% of UK people aged 25–34 believe that reality shows are 100% real.
3. What about talent shows, one of the most popular kinds of reality programme? Did you know that it's usually the producers of the show — not the judges — who actually decide who gets eliminated? Talent is often far less important than other things. The producers want contestants who attract more viewers, for example by starting big arguments. In fact, some producers deliberately create drama and tension between the contestants.
4. Does it really matter that reality shows aren't real? In some documentaries, they say they're in one place when they're actually filming somewhere completely different. In other shows, they "surprise" the contestants with things the contestants already knew about. And in most shows, time itself is fake: an activity that really takes hours, days, or weeks somehow happens in a 50-minute episode.
5. One of the saddest things is that reality shows can give us unrealistic ideas about what we ourselves can do. Things aren't as fast as they look on the screen, and they're usually a bit more complicated. Reality shows can make us confused, because we can't tell the difference between what's real and what isn't. The scariest thing is that they can make us stop trusting everything we see on TV — including the news. People start thinking it's all manipulation. Maybe the best idea is to stop watching reality TV for a while and rediscover the real world. It's just as amazing and inspiring as anything you can see on a screen.

In your opinion — does reality TV have a negative influence on us?

Use ideas from the text and from your own experience. Try to use at least three adjectives from the flip cards. Speak for about a minute.

arguments YES — it's harmful
  • It can give unrealistic ideas about what we can do.
  • Some shows are violent or manipulative.
  • Producers create fake drama on purpose.
  • It can make us stop trusting the news too.
arguments NO — it's fine
  • It's entertaining and helps people relax.
  • Some shows are genuinely inspiring (cooking, dance, talent).
  • Viewers know it's edited — they aren't stupid.
  • The best shows can be moving and informative.
