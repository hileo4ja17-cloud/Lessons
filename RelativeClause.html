2

Defining relative clauses

~ 20 minutes · the rule
the rule

What is a defining clause?

A defining relative clause tells us which person or thing we are talking about. The information is necessary: without it, the sentence loses meaning.

  • 👉 It identifies the noun — which one?
  • 👉 It uses who, which, that, where, when, whose.
  • 👉 No commas around the clause.
  • 👉 That can replace who or which (very common in spoken English).
  • 👉 If the pronoun is the object, you can leave it out.
The girl who lives next door is my best friend.
→ Which girl? The one who lives next door. We need this information!

4

Non-defining relative clauses

~ 20 minutes · extra info, with care
the rule

What is a non-defining clause?

A non-defining clause gives us extra information. It is not necessary — if we remove it, the sentence still makes sense.

  • 👉 The information is extra — like a comment.
  • 👉 Always between commas (or after a comma at the end).
  • 👉 We cannot use “that”. Only who, which, where, when, whose.
  • 👉 We cannot omit the pronoun.
  • 👉 Often used in writing and more formal speech.
My sister, who lives in Paris, is a doctor.
→ I have only one sister. The fact she lives in Paris is just extra information.

Defining vs non-defining — at a glance

D Defining

  • Tells us which one
  • Necessary information
  • No commas
  • Can use that
  • Can omit pronoun (object)
  • Spoken & written

N Non-defining

  • Gives extra information
  • Not necessary
  • Always commas
  • Cannot use that
  • Cannot omit pronoun
  • More formal/written

A tiny trick 🪄

If you can put the relative clause inside brackets ( ) — like a quiet little comment — it is non-defining. If removing it changes the meaning, it is defining.

Warsaw, which is the capital of Poland, is a beautiful city.
→ Warsaw is a beautiful city. (still makes sense → non-defining ✓)

More examples · with commas

notice the pauses 🌿
people · who
My mother, who is a teacher, makes the best cookies.
things · which
The Eiffel Tower, which is in Paris, is very famous.
places · where
Krakow, where my grandparents live, is full of history.
time · when
In 2020, when the world stopped, I learnt to bake bread.
possession · whose
My brother, whose dog is huge, lives in Berlin.
whole idea · which
She forgot my birthday, which made me sad.

What is the difference between defining and non-defining clauses?
A defining clause says which one (necessary, no commas). A non-defining clause gives extra info (always commas, no that, no omission).
When can I leave the pronoun out?
Only in defining clauses, when the pronoun is the object. Example: The book (that) I read…
Can I use “that” in a non-defining clause?
No 🚫. In non-defining clauses we use only who, which, where, when, whose.
